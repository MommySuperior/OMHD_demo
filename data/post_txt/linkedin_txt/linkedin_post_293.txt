I’m pleased to share a new publication in the Journal of Sport and Social Issues:
Mental Health Marketing through Sport: A Point–Counterpoint Discussion. In this dialogue paper, Beth Cianfrone and I bring together sport marketing and sport sociology perspectives to examine the growing presence of mental health sponsorships and endorsements in sport. While these partnerships are often framed as stigma-reducing and socially responsible, they also raise important ethical and theoretical questions related to branding, neoliberalism, and the commodification of mental health care.

We ask questions like: 
-Do athlete endorsements and organizational sponsorships of mental health companies reduce stigma or repackage it?
-What happens when care becomes a branded, for-profit product?
-Who actually benefits from these partnerships?

This paper is meant to spark conversation across disciplines and between scholars, practitioners, and sport leaders. We’d love for you to read it, and we'd love to hear your thoughts.
