🚀 Our new umbrella review (i.e., a review of reviews) led by Lasse Brandt and Jurjen Luykx on climate-related and nature-based interventions for mental health is out in JAMA Psychiatry

🔎 Despite growing evidence on the impacts of climate change on mental health, we still know little about what evidence-based interventions should be implemented to address these mental health outcomes

💡 This was largely confirmed by the current umbrella review which found limited evidence for mental health interventions to reduce adverse mental health impacts of climatic impact drivers

🌲 I was particularly surprised to see that the review failed to identify systematic reviews and/or meta-analyses of nature-based interventions aimed at reducing the adverse mental health impacts of natural environment loss, increased temperatures, air pollution, and other climatic impact drivers

🌱 While the authors highlights that there is promising potential for future research in this field based on evidence from contexts other than climate change, this paper is another call to action concerning the need for more evidence on mental health interventions for contexts affected by climate change
