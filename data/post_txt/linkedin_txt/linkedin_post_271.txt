This Mental Health Awareness Month, we recognize both the scale of the need and the opportunity to make a meaningful difference. Across California, more than 6.3 million people are living with a mental health condition. For many, finding timely, consistent support after a crisis remains difficult. The first 72 hours are especially important for stabilization and recovery, yet nationally only 30–45% of people receive follow-up care within the next week. Through the @Anthem Blue Cross Foundation, we’re helping strengthen community-based organizations expanding access to mental health resources and care across California, including $3.7 million in grant funding to partners.
 
One example is @Downtown Women's Center, which provides mental health care and substance use support to women experiencing homelessness in Los Angeles. Through this work, 2,807 individual and group counseling sessions were delivered, 1,105 people were reached through outreach events and workshops, and 42% reduction in women's unsheltered homelessness in Skid Row was achieved. When care is easier to access and navigate, people can get support sooner, stay connected longer, and experience better outcomes. https://lnkd.in/gFiqQBxP
