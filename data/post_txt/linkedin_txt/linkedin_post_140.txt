What if the future of mental health was your neighborhood, your park, your community, becoming a hub for healing?

Through Boyz N The Wood and HBCUs Outside, Ronald V. E. Griswell and I are expanding networks of care that are rooted in nature and grow from within the community itself. We (and our members, partners, and collaborators all across the country) are reimagining what care can look like when it’s peer-led, culturally rooted, and built in relationship with nature. 

What if we didn’t wait for systems to catch up or hide healing behind paywalls?

This work challenges long-standing barriers to mental health access and outdoor inclusion for Black Americans, proving that healing doesn’t have to depend on or wait for broken systems to be fixed. We can heal our communities, and we can do it together.

At TEDxJacksonville 2026: On the Edge, Ron and I will add to the story of how community-designed and nature-centered healing infrastructure may be one of the most powerful paths forward for our humanity and society today.

Join us January 17 at the University of North Florida, or stay tuned for the video of our talk.
