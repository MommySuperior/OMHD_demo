Everyone accessing mental health and wellbeing services should feel safe, respected and heard.  

The dignity and autonomy principle means your rights and choices are understood, protected and taken seriously.  

You can involve your family, carers or supporters as much as you choose, stay connected with the people who matter to you, and speak up if something doesn’t feel right. 

It means services make all reasonable efforts to help keep carers and families informed, included and valued, while supporting the person to make their own decisions.

To read more about the dignity and autonomy principle, visit https://lnkd.in/ghSn3sgP
