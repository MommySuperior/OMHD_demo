May is Mental Health Awareness Month. The theme this year is "More Good Days, Together."

A lot of people in our industry, and in every industry, are carrying things you do not see. Some of it is health, some is family, some is money — the kind of weight that does not announce itself in meetings. Some people are in real difficulty, some are quietly doing well, most are somewhere in between, and it shifts week to week.

The work still has to get done. That part does not change, and pretending otherwise is not fair to anyone. But how we treat each other while it gets done is the thing this month is really pointing at. It looks like asking sincere questions, giving people room to be honest when they need it, and paying attention not as a program but as a habit.

Resilience is not the absence of struggle. It is what people can do when they feel supported.
