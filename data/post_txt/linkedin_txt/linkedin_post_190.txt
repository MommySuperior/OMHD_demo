As we near the end of Mental Health Awareness Month, I’d like to reflect on a cause that I care deeply about. 
 
After we moved our family from Houston to Boston about 8 years ago, my wife, Masu, and I got involved with The Brookline Center for Community Mental Health, a local resource that provides affordable mental health care and community-based social services to individuals and families in Massachusetts. Years later, my wife now serves on the Center’s board as its strategic development chair and we have chaired their Kinds Fund gala, deepening our connection to the Center and its mission. 
 
My wife and I felt called to support the need for accessible mental health care following the pandemic. As parents, we saw firsthand how isolation and the loss of connection greatly impacted social development and relationship building, especially among young people. 
 
While this month helps raise awareness, the responsibility to listen, support one another, and build stronger communities extends far beyond May. I’m proud to support the critical, lifesaving care the Brookline Center provides in my local community, and to bring that same commitment to EVgo by supporting the wellbeing of our team through employee mental health initiatives.
