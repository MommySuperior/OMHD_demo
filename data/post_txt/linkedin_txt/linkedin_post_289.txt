Mental Health Awareness Month feels especially personal this year.

Robin and I have been reminded again, through people close to us and in our local community, how deeply mental health challenges affect individuals, families and everyone around them.

In moments of loss, people often look for one answer. One event. One thing that “triggered” what happened. I understand that instinct. It comes from grief and the need to make sense of something painful. But I also think it shows how far we still have to go in the way we talk about mental health.
Mental health is health. It deserves the same seriousness, compassion and support we bring to conditions like heart disease or diabetes. It is not weakness. It is not something people should have to carry alone.

At Chiesi USA, Inc., I’m grateful we continue to build a culture where asking for support is encouraged and where resources are available for employees and families. But this is bigger than any one workplace. It is about changing the conversation with more honesty, less stigma and more care.

If you are struggling, please reach out to someone. You are not alone.
