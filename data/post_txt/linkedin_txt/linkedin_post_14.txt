Across public health, mental health, outdoor recreation, and policy spaces, there is growing recognition that improving wellbeing in this country will require us to think differently about prevention, connection, and care.

Next week, I’ll join Senator Michael Bennet, Catherine Price, and Jessica (Wahl) Turner at Outside Days for a conversation on:
“The Outdoor Prescription: Health, Access, and Public Policy.”

Outdoor Recreation Roundtable’s Health Vision is helping push that conversation forward by positioning outdoor access and recreation as part of America’s broader health infrastructure. And Senator Michael Bennet’s work on expanding access to mental and behavioral healthcare calls us to “reconsider how we can bring local communities into the continuum of care.” 

Together, these ideas reflect a growing understanding that healing cannot rest solely inside clinical systems.

At Boyz N The Wood, we’ve witnessed firsthand how nature, brotherhood, and trusted community can create conditions for healing, especially for Black men navigating stress, isolation, and disconnection.

That experience has led us to larger questions:
 • What would it look like to equip local communities as key components in our continuum of care? 
 • What would it look like for government, healthcare, philanthropy, outdoor recreation, and public agencies to invest in that infrastructure together?

These are not just theoretical questions for us. We’re asking and helping to shape the answer through our Colorado Communities of Care pilot, a place-based effort exploring how outdoor access, mental health support, research, and community leadership can work together as part of a broader health ecosystem.

This conversation is growing across sectors. Excited to both discuss the idea on the panel, and work on bringing it to life.
