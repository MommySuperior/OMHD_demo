Gen Alpha is entering mental health care earlier. And they expect it to work the first time. 

It’s been reassuring to see this shift in my clinical practice, where parents and kids are 1) seeking care at the first onset of symptoms instead of waiting to be in a crisis, and 2) assessing for fit in the first visit. It’s even more exciting to see this shift on a national level when we explored our own Headway data on hundreds of thousands of young patients. 

Huge thanks to Bloomberg for including Headway’s perspective on what this shift signals for the future of care. What we’re seeing isn’t just increased awareness; it’s earlier engagement, higher expectations for fit and quality, and less tolerance for broken systems.

At Headway, with nearly 70,000 clinicians serving millions of patients, we’re able to see these patterns at scale and translate our learnings into a better infrastructure: stronger matching, earlier intervention, and more durable outcomes.

As a clinician, this is the future I came here to help build.
