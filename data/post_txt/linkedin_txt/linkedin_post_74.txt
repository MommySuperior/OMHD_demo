The National Action Plan on Mental Health and Criminal Justice launched yesterday by The Mental Health Commission of Canada is the result of years of sustained, courageous, and deeply human work. A remarkable group of advisors — from Indigenous communities and organizations, from lived and living experience, from law, corrections, health, academia, and advocacy — came together and stayed the course. 

The intersection of mental health and criminal justice is one of the most complex in our entire social system. And complexity, here, is not an abstraction. It is a person.

There is no single system, no single sector, no single level of government that holds the answer alone. Health, justice, corrections, housing, social services, community organizations, Indigenous governments and communities, people with lived experience — all have a role to play. All are named. All are called upon.
