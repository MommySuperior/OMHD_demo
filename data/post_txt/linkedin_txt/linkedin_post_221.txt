May is Mental Health Awareness Month. During this time, it’s important to remember that mental health is health.

The stigma surrounding mental health often keeps people from reaching out, but there is no shame in seeking support.  If you or someone you know is struggling with mental or behavioral health, it is important to get assistance or treatment. 

The Suffolk County Department of Health Services has a dedicated staff in the Division of Community Mental Hygiene Services whose role is to assist people with mental illness and substance use disorders connect to services and treatment options in Suffolk County.

There are many ways a person can reach out for support including:
• Call or text 988 to chat with a member of the lifeline 24/7 who can provide support or services.
• If you need a safe place to go in person, visit FSL-DASH, located at 90 Adams Ave. in Hauppauge or call 631-650-0110.
• Contact the FSL Mobile Crisis Team at 631-952-3333 who can provide support over the phone or come to you.
• Access the Suffolk County Directory of Behavioral Health Services website 24/7 to find services that include prevention, harm reduction, treatment, recovery and support.
• For non-urgent assistance, contact the 311 Call Center by dialing 311 to connect with someone who can direct you to the appropriate services.
• For hospital-based emergency psychiatric services, call Stony Brook Medicine CPEP at 631-444-2990
• If someone is a danger to themselves or someone else, call 911.

For more information on the Suffolk County Division of Community Mental Hygiene Services, visit https://lnkd.in/ebYBNHFz or call 631-853-8500.
