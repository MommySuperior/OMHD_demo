Who is responsible when institutional policies harm faculty mental health? 

💭 An ethical question we don’t ask in Indian higher education

In many Indian engineering institutions, faculty are required to call parents when students are absent or underperforming.

But what happens when those calls turn abusive?

And more importantly:
If a faculty member’s mental health is seriously harmed while following an institutional mandate, who is ethically responsible?

The faculty member for “not coping”?

Or the institution that enforced the policy without safeguards?


Mental health cannot be conditional on compliance. No professional duty should require enduring abuse.

Most institutions claim to have support mechanisms—counseling cells, mentors, grievance committees. In reality, many faculty hesitate to use them due to stigma, fear of appraisal impact, or lack of clear protocols to disengage after abuse.

When harm is foreseeable and preventable, responsibility shifts upward.

At what point does institutional compliance become ethical complicity in harm?

Would genuinely value perspectives from faculty, academic leaders, and policymakers.
