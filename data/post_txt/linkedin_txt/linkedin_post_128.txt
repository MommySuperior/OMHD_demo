Mental Health Awareness Month in May

The Frederick County Mental Health Court Team links its participants, people charged with misdemeanor offenses, to critical resources resulting in their decreased recidivism, improved quality of life and reduced reliance on psychiatric hospitalization.  
The Team includes the Presiding Judge, Frederick County State’s Attorney’s Office, MD Office of the Public Defender, Division of Parole and Probation, Mental Health Court Coordinator, Frederick Police Department  and the Frederick County Health Department.  
We thank the wonderful mental health community organizations for providing their services and we celebrated our most recent graduating class on March 11, 2026! 
Chief of the District Court, Assistant State’s Attorney, Kristin King, is committed to accommodating the voluminous yet uniquely specialized docket for these cases.  Referrals to the program can be accepted from defendants themselves, community organizations, law enforcement, attorneys, family members and more!
The District Court of Maryland for Frederick County’s Mental Health Court is a specialized intervention program designed for individuals diagnosed with a mental health condition and charged with misdemeanor offenses. This voluntary program aims to connect participants with appropriate treatment and support services within the community, with the goal of enhancing overall quality of life.
The Mental Health Court embodies a collaborative approach among justice system professionals committed to deepening their understanding of mental health issues. Participants are assigned to a specialized docket overseen by the Mental Health Court team, which delivers a non-adversarial, individualized process tailored to meet each participant’s unique needs.
For more information regarding this program, please contact the Mental Health Court Coordinator at 301-600-2656.
