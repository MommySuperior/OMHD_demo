Thank you Ami Bhatt, MD for this thoughtful synthesis and situating our findings within the broader conversation on integrated and digital care. I completely agree, mental health is not merely a comorbidity but part of the cardiovascular risk pathway itself. One of the key takeaways from our work is that stress-related brain activity, autonomic imbalance, and inflammation provide a biological bridge between depression/anxiety and cardiovascular disease, helping explain why these conditions, especially when they co-occur, carry meaningful cardiometabolic risk. 

Your point about design responsibility is especially important: digital mental health tools that overlook downstream cardiovascular implications risk missing a critical prevention opportunity. 

This aligns closely with our recent discussion in Nature Reviews Cardiology on the need to integrate mental and cardiovascular health as part of a unified prevention framework. Integrated care isn’t aspirational, it’s necessary.

Grateful for your leadership in advancing this perspective.
