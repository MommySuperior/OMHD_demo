The Mental Health Commission has launched new guidance aimed at strengthening the quality, safety and accessibility of mental health services for adults and children with intellectual disabilities. 

The purpose of this guidance is to support staff in delivering care and treatment to individuals with co-occurring mental ill-health and intellectual disabilities across all mental health service settings for adults and children, including inpatient units, community residences and community-based services. 

It promotes a person-centred approach that upholds and protects human rights, ensuring that care is inclusive, responsive and respectful of individual needs. In doing so, it supports services to continually build their capability, enhance professional confidence, and embed practices that lead to better outcomes for individuals and families.
