Mental health is not a luxury, it is a necessity, especially for those navigating vulnerability, uncertainty, and systemic neglect.
On May 20, Tdh is co-hosting an event dedicated to mental health, bringing together voices and perspectives that are too often overlooked. This discussion is part of a broader series of events we are organizing in the coming months to shed light on the very real consequences of political decisions, particularly funding cuts and the deprioritization of critical issues. Behind every budget line are lives impacted. Vulnerable groups are often the first to feel these shifts, yet their needs remain the least visible in policy debates. Through this event, we aim to create space for dialogue, reflection, and, importantly, action. Raising awareness is only the first step, what follows must be a renewed commitment to protecting and prioritizing mental health for all. Join us on May 20!
