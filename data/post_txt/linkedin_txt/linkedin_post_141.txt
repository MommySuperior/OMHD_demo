For Mental Health Awareness Month, I wanted to share a PCORI-funded study that compared outcomes for individuals assigned to treatment with a common antidepressant or with mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR), a type of meditation.

Both groups saw similar reductions in anxiety symptoms. However, nearly 80% of participants taking medication experienced side effects — compared to only 15% of those practicing MBSR. Patients and clinicians can use these results when considering treatments for anxiety disorders.
