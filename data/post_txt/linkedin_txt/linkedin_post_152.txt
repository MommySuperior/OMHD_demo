Maternal mental health is not a side issue in maternal health. It is a driver of outcomes.

In our Safe Babies Safe Moms work, we saw clearly that you cannot improve maternal and infant outcomes without directly addressing perinatal mental health.

Yet too often, systems are designed to screen without the infrastructure to treat.

This is why we built an integrated perinatal mental health model within Safe Babies Safe Moms that does more than identify need. It responds to it.

Our approach connects screening, clinical care, behavioral health, and social support into a coordinated system across pregnancy, birth, and postpartum.

Because identifying depression, anxiety, or trauma is only the first step.
What matters is what the system does next.

In maternal health, gaps in follow-up are not just inefficiencies. They are risks.
When mental health care is embedded into the structure of care delivery, not treated as an add-on, we begin to see what is possible.

Better engagement
Earlier intervention
Improved outcomes for both mother and baby

Maternal mental health is not separate from maternal safety. It is central to it.

That is the work we are continuing to build through Safe Babies Safe Moms.
