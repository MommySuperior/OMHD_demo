As highlighted in recent OECD reports on adolescent wellbeing, mental health must be at the centre of prevention programmes for young people.

The European Prevention Curriculum (EUPC) also reminds us that effective prevention is not simply about substances themselves, but about strengthening protective factors in people’s lives.

Learning to live with others, accept frustrations, know ones emetions and learn how to regulate, manage difficulties, and build one’s own pathway are essential elements of positive socialisation. These experiences help adolescents develop resilience, autonomy, and a sense of purpose — key foundations for healthy and fulfilling lives.

Prevention is about creating the conditions for young people to grow into adults who can face challenges, build healthy relationships, and move forward without relying on harmful coping mechanisms. 

Investing in mental wellbeing means investing in stronger, healthier, and more confident generations.
