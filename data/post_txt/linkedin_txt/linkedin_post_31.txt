May is Mental Health Awareness Month.

I’ve been pretty open over the years about my own experience: it’s real, it’s common, and it deserves the same attention we give anything else in our life that impacts our ability to live rich and rewarding lives.

There’s a version of our jobs, and frankly, a version of this world, that rewards pushing through, ignoring the signals in your gut, and telling yourself you’ll deal with it later. I’ve done that. It doesn’t work for long. 

So my message is simple: Talk openly about mental health. Normalize it. Take it seriously. Make time for your mental health in the same way you would any other health problem.  

Building things that last means making sure you’re around and healthy to see that vision come to life.
