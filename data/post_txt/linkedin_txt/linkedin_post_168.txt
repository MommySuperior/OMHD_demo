The Mental Health Commission welcomes the passing of the Mental Health Bill 2024 through both Houses of the Oireachtas. Once the Bill has been formally signed into law by President Catherine Connolly – thus becoming the Mental Health Act 2026 – it will represent the first significant change to Irish mental health laws in more than quarter of a century.

The new Act will see the expansion of the regulatory function of the MHC to include all community mental health residences and services, including all community Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services.

MHC Chief Executive John Farrelly, said “By expanding the regulatory function of the Mental Health Commission to include all community mental health residences and services, including all community CAMHS, the new Act has further protected the rights of people with mental health difficulties."

“Both Ministers Butler [Minister for Mental Health] and Carroll MacNeill [Minister for Health], and their teams, deserve our recognition and appreciation for advancing modern and progressive legislation that will enhance the voice of patients and create more safeguards for service users."
