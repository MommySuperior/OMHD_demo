May the years ahead mark a paradigm shift in moving beyond RCTs/trials to use the tools of implementation science to test strategies for scaling and sustaining with quality the many evidence-based MHPSS interventions that have been developed and evaluated in a range of cultures and contexts in the past 30 years. 

By putting forth and pursuing research questions under the auspices of implementation science (i.e. testing optimal strategies for training and supervision of nonspecialist MHPSS providers, integration of evidence-based MHPSS into existing systems, methods for increasing buy in and localization of evidence-based services, policy approaches to increasing budgetary and workforce investments in MHPSS) we can make these shifts as a field. Not only will a paradigm shift help us achieve greater success in the translation of our scientific progress in global MHPSS, but working with local partners we can strengthen and sustain local systems of MHPSS and social services in war-affected settings globally.  According to the most recent data from UNICEF and Save the Children, approximately 520 million children now live in active conflict zones--we have no time to waste!
