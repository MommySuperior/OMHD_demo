Mental Health Awareness Week starts today, and this year's theme, 'Action', is a direct invitation to do something, not just mark the occasion.

One of the most practical things you can do this week is take free online suicide awareness training. On Tuesday, 12 May (1–2.30 pm), thriveldn and zero-suicide-alliance are running a 90-minute session covering how to recognise warning signs, start a direct conversation, use appropriate language, and signpost someone to professional support.

The training is funded by the MayorofLondon and open to anyone — whether you work in the NHS, a local authority, a community organisation, or simply want to feel more prepared to support people in your own life. No prior experience or specialist knowledge is needed.

When thriveldn ran the same session in January, 98% of the 400 people who gave feedback said they'd recommend it to a friend or colleague. It received an average quality rating of 4.7 out of 5.
