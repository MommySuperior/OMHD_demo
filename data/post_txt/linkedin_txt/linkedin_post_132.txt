Expanding access to mental health care requires creating clear, more efficient pathways to licensure.

For many talented people with master’s degrees in mental health, the hardest part comes after graduation. The journey to licensure can be long, expensive, and isolating. This is where future clinicians too often get stuck and some make the decision to step away.
 
That is why we launched the Kaiser Permanente Mental Health Career Accelerator in partnership with the National Council for Mental Wellbeing. 

The program focuses on supporting aspiring clinicians at this critical stage, with financial incentives, training, mentorship, and connection. More than 160 graduates have already participated, and we are building on what we have learned as the program continues to grow.  
 
This conversation continues at #NatCon26, where I look forward to connecting with candidates, advisors, and partners, and sharing how we can rethink mental health care delivery to better align with how people actually live.
