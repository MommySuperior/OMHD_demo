📣 New Report alert!

The mental health system isn't broken because of a lack of will but because its architecture hasn't kept pace with need. A new framework from Dr. Benjamin Miller and The Bridgespan Group Group sets out to change that.

"Expanding the Mental Health Care Continuum: A Framework for Philanthropic Investment in the Mental Health Workforce" makes the case for three essential shifts:
👉 Individual shift: Equipping everyday people (barbers, coaches, librarians, teachers) with practical helping skills.
👉 Community shift: Building a robust, culturally competent community-based workforce.
👉 Clinical shift: Integrating licensed mental health care into the clinical settings where people already seek help.

Tied together by a coordination and infrastructure layer, these shifts form a new continuum of care: one that catches people earlier, reaches further, and reduces the bottleneck on an overstretched clinical system.

This is the kind of systems-level thinking that philanthropy is uniquely positioned to back. Not more programmes at the margins but the conditions that allow proven models to scale and stick.
