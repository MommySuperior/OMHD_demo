Ryan Travia, Ed.D., FACHA, Marcus Hotaling and I are excited to share the call for proposals for a new NASPA publication, "Transforming Mental Health in Higher Education: Leadership Strategies and Innovative Approaches".

We invite chapter proposals for a forthcoming edited book examining leadership strategies and innovative approaches to mental health service delivery in higher education, with a particular focus on post-pandemic transformations in counseling center operations, institutional mental health frameworks, and community-based approaches to student well-being.

This comprehensive volume will analyze contemporary challenges including mental health policy implications, evidence-based treatment modalities, crisis intervention protocols, resource allocation strategies, and the pedagogical integration of faculty as mental health allies within institutional ecosystems.

We seek empirically grounded contributions from counseling center directors, student affairs practitioners, faculty scholars, and clinical researchers who can provide theoretical frameworks, methodological innovations, and applied research findings that advance our understanding of contemporary collegiate mental health service delivery. The book will synthesize scholarship with practitioner wisdom through various sections, while examining institutional variability across sectors (public, private, community colleges, etc.). 

We are committed to centering diverse scholarly voices and identities to ensure representation in this essential resource for mental health research, policy, and practice in higher education. Please note that authors of accepted proposals may be paired with a co-author(s) to ensure the broadest representation possible and are subject to final approval by NASPA.
