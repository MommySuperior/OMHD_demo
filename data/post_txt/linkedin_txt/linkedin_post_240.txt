NCDHHS Invests $20 Million in Non-Law Enforcement Transportation for Mental Health Crises 

NCDHHS has released a RFP to establish non-law enforcement transportation services for those with mental health and substance use crisis needs.  

This initiative provides trauma-informed transportation from emergency rooms to treatment facilities, offering a safe and therapeutic alternative to traditional law enforcement transport. 

By decriminalizing and destigmatizing the process of seeking mental health care, we aim to encourage more individuals to access the support they need without fear. 

The program will initially operate in two regions, selected through a competitive request for proposal (RFP) process. 

Eligible transportation vendors can apply for the RFP via the NCDHHS website here: https://lnkd.in/eeibQ-4G  

This effort reflects our commitment to transforming mental health crisis response in North Carolina, ensuring compassionate care for all.
