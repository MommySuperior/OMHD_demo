May is Mental Health Awareness Month. For many people — myself included — living with mental health challenges can feel like a silent battle. At times, seeking and receiving support feels possible. But more often, it can feel out of reach. Stigma, financial barriers, isolation, and limited access to care are only a few of the obstacles that so many know all too well.

In my role at HealthRight International, my motivation for strengthening mental health care systems begins as a personal one, but it's fueled knowing that so many others face similar struggles. I will always advocate that mental health needs to be approached from a community-based perspective and integrated with all aspects of health. How am I doing this? It starts with meeting communities where they are. But the true action is actually shifting the paradigm of how we think about mental health care: breaking down stigma through psychoeducation, training trusted members in the community in delivering low-intensity interventions, and developing sustainable financial models beyond Medicaid billing that make both accessing and providing care feasible over the long term.
