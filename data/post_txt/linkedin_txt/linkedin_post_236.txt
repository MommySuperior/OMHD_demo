For too long, mental health and disability services have operated in separate conversations. The reality is that people deserve support that recognizes the whole person and responds to their experiences, goals, and needs in a connected way.

This is an important conversation, especially as more communities work to improve access to trauma-informed behavioral health support and reduce barriers to care.

I appreciate The Baltimore Sun for publishing this important piece by Elisabeth Turner, LCSW-C, Director of Behavioral Health at Fello, highlighting the intersection of mental health and disabilities and why integrated support matters.
