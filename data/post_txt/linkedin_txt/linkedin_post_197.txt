The good news is that about 54% of employees (2024) feel their employer cares about their mental health (up 6% from 2023). The bad news is that almost 40% of employees report high stress at work. This translates into an added $5.3 million in cost annually for every 1,000 employees, which is pretty staggering. These costs hit 3 main areas: cost control (higher health claims), risk mitigation (making mistakes, missing deadlines, cutting corners), and sustained performance (increased sick days, lower engagement). Are we asking the right questions of our people? Questions such as:” How often do you feel stressed, anxious, or overwhelmed at work?” Followed by: “To what extent do your workload and deadlines feel manageable?” And” Do you feel psychologically safe raising concerns or asking for help?” I think the best line in this article is “Resilience isn’t a program- it is a system embedded into how organizations lead, operate and grow.”  Perhaps the most important question of all is one we ask ourselves as leaders: “How are we doing?”
