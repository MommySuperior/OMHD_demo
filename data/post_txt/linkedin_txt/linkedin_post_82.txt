🚨 New esports mental health editorial 🚨

"Time to Monitor the Mental Health of Esports Athletes" is now published in the British Journal of Sports Medicine (BJSM).

Over the past few years, esports has attracted a level of institutional recognition that would have seemed unlikely a decade ago, from the International Olympic Committee – IOC's Olympic Esports Series and Esports Week to the Esports World Cup and Esports Nations Cup (Esports Foundation), which have drawn millions of viewers globally. The competitive infrastructure around esports players is growing fast.

But institutional recognition without a corresponding duty of care is an incomplete step forward. Here, we argue that stakeholders in sports medicine, public health, and esports must work together to extend structured mental health monitoring to those competing on digital fields.

💡 The core argument:

Esports athletes train and compete under conditions that closely mirror those of traditional sports, including high-performance pressure, public scrutiny, short contracts, and blurred boundaries between training and recovery. Anxiety symptoms have been reported in 38–82% of players, and depressive symptoms in 25–37% in some samples. Yet unlike athletes in traditional sports, most esports players have no formal mental health surveillance system watching over them.

The sports medicine community has already built tools and frameworks, such as the IOC consensus statement (Margo Mountjoy, Scott Sloan, Vincent Gouttebarge) on mental health and the Sports Mental Health Assessment Tool (SMHAT), that could, with adaptation, be extended to esports. Early initiatives from Riot Games, BLAST, The GG Project, and The FUSE Cup - International School Esports show integration is feasible. Alongside these, organisations such as Movember have been investing directly in esports-specific research and programs.

What's missing is coordinated leadership to make it standard practice.

Mental health monitoring is not an optional enhancement. It is a foundational component of athlete care, and esports athletes deserve the same standard.
