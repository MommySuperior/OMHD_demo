The stigma around mental health isn’t dying just because of awareness campaigns. It’s dying because high performers have started talking too.

Someone fractures their wrist at work.

Everyone rallies :
“Go home” 
“Rest” 
“We’ve got you”

But when someone says they’re not okay mentally - that they’re running on empty, something feels off inside, the room gets a little quiet.

Nothing cruel gets said, but something shifts.

And that gap, between how we treat a broken bone and a broken spirit, is what I think about most.

Here’s what’s actually changing though : 

The conversation is louder than it’s ever been. More founders, leaders, athletes and high performers are saying out loud - I needed support and I got it and I’m better for it. Not as confession, but as a fact.

The most self-aware people I know don’t wait for a breakdown to invest in their mental fitness. They treat it like a non-negotiable. Like training, and strategy.

That reframe is everything.

It moves mental health out of just the crisis conversation and also puts it into the performance conversation. Out of shame and into self growth.

The stigma isn’t completely gone, but it’s losing ground fast.

And the people leading that shift aren’t just therapists or mental health/wellness advocates.

They’re also the high performers who decided that taking care of their mind was the smartest professional decision they ever made.

That energy is contagious, and I think we’re at a tipping point.
