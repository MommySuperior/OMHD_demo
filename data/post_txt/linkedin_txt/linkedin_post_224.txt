May is Mental Health Awareness Month, a time to pause and reflect on something that impacts all of us. 

We all have a mix of good days and hard days, and it’s okay to not be okay. This month is a reminder to take care of ourselves, support one another, and make use of the resources available to us. 
 
Personally, I’ve found that the simplest habits often make the biggest difference. For me, that’s taking a few minutes to step away between meetings or getting outside for a quick walk to reset. It doesn’t solve everything, but it helps me show up more focused and present. I know everyone’s approach looks different, but I encourage you to find what works for you.  
 
Each year, I join the CAMH (Centre for Addiction and Mental Health Foundation) Sunrise Challenge, where we “rise together for mental health.” It’s a simple but powerful act: starting the day with intention and sharing a sunrise photo. For me, this ritual reinforces the importance of prioritizing mental well-being, and I’m grateful to have taken on this challenge alongside so many colleagues from our Canada team over the years. 
 
Let’s take the time this month to prioritize our mental health and support each other in doing the same.
