Announcing Eggshells, a virtual, trauma-informed mental health program for Veteran Families in Canada impacted by substance use.

Families impacted by substance use describe family life as perpetually walking on eggshells. With this in mind, this new evidence-based program, designed exclusively for Veteran Families, combines counselling and addictions expertise with military and Veteran lived experience to provide virtual group counselling and peer support.

Funded by the Veterans Affairs Canada Veteran and Family Well-being Fund, Eggshells offers a comprehensive (no cost) program that includes;

• 24 clinical care hours (virtual group counselling) plus
• 16 continuing care hours (virtual peer support).

Participants affected by a loved one’s substance use will unpack their emotions and examine their behaviours. Together, groups will explore family dynamics, healthy boundaries, effective communications, self-care, and much more.

Eggshells is available to Veteran Family members regardless of whether their loved one is in active addiction, seeking or receiving treatment, in recovery, or if they are grieving the loss of their loved one.

Eggshells is available to Veterans or a Veteran spouse/partner, parent, adult child or sibling, extended family member or chosen family (friend or peer).

Eggshells aims to enhance family well-being and strengthen the entire family system through trauma-informed care. This unique program integrates military culture, lifestyle, and the diverse factors that influence Veteran and Veteran Family health and well-being.

There is no cost for qualified Veteran Families to participate in the program. *Note: Eggshells is not a treatment program for substance use.
