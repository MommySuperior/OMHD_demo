This week, a roundtable on digital mental health was held in Colombo, co-hosted by the Prime Minister’s Office and the eMental Health International Collaborative. This brought together clinicians and community practitioners, persons with lived experience of MH conditions, researchers, innovation and industry representatives, policy makers and govt officials to discuss the state of digital mental health in Sri Lanka and its future. 

In her keynote address, PM Dr.Harini Amarasuriya spoke about the need to prioritise the following in digital mental health innovation for Sri Lanka: Accessibility and Equity, Quality and Safety, Participation of persons with lived experience, Cost-Effectiveness and Integration with existing systems for Scalability and Sustainability. She also expressed the need for, “regulatory frameworks that encourage innovation while ensuring efficacy, safety, data privacy, security, and other ethical considerations”.

These considerations were echoed in the discussions that followed in small group exchanges between the diverse participants - many of whom were meeting  for the first time, despite their overlapping interests in the area. There were many connections made and offers of support were made both bilaterally between participants and to the wider group to help take both conversation and actions forward.

During the roundtable, we also had the opportunity to hear Senator Katherine (Kathy) Hay ICD.D of Canada speak about the principles and approaches that underpinned the transformation of the Kids Help Phone services that she led over the past 7 years, and also hear Prof Andrew Greenshaw and Prof Anil Thapliyal brief us on the ongoing rapid developments in digital mental health globally.

I’m very hopeful for what will come from this meeting and the conversation that has started amongst diverse stakeholders who will need to work together to advance digital mental health in SL.
