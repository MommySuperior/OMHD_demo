May is Mental Health Awareness Month: a time to pause, reflect, and remember that mental well-being matters every day, in every space. 

It’s also a time to break stigma, share resources, and reaffirm that conditions like anxiety, depression, and trauma deserve understanding,
silence.

At the Trevor Noah Foundation, we believe this is especially true in our schools. Learning can only truly happen when learners and teachers feel safe, supported, and emotionally grounded.

Through our partnership with @MyOnlineTherapist, our Khulani Schools Programme provides access to trained Care Agents who walk alongside learners and teachers through the everyday realities of school life, offering consistent, compassionate psychosocial support when it’s needed most.

Because mental health is not a moment, it is a commitment we carry throughout.
