Mental health affects us all, and no one should feel alone in their struggles. This Mental Health Awareness Month, the City of Huntington Beach joins communities across the nation in recognizing the importance of mental wellness and support.

This year’s theme, “Mental Health Is
healing and support can often be found in our everyday lives, through our communities, relationships, cultures, routines, and the people around us.

According to the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration:
• 1 in 5 adults experiences a mental health condition each year
• 1 in 6 youth experience a major depressive episode
• Suicide remains one of the leading causes of death among young people ages 10–24

If you or someone you know is struggling, help is available. Local mental health resources are available through the Orange County Health Care Agency at
ocnavigator.org/m/ocn
or call 855-625-4657. 

And remember, the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline is available 24/7, just call or text 988 for immediate support.

Remember that mental health matters.
