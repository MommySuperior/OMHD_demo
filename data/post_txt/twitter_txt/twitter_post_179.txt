Recognizing the need for preventative mental health assistance programs and aid for deputies responding to mental health or substance abuse crisis calls,
@
SheriffStaly
established the Behavioral Response Unit last year. The BRU responds with deputies, assists in crisis
ven helped train K-9 handlers to quickly find missing endangered persons.

We're proud of our team of Mental Health Specialists, who have responded to over 500 calls for service and provided care coordination for over 60 individuals within the past year. Here is just a glimpse of the important work they do ⬇️

💚 Last fall, BRU Mental Health Specialist, Megan Ionadi, joined patrol units at the scene of a suicide threat in which a female had discharged a firearm inside a residence while she was extremely
intoxicated and threatening to commit suicide. A member of FCSO’s Crisis Negotiation Team (CNT) tried to make contact with the woman, but she said she was not willing to speak with Law
Enforcement. 

Instead, Ionadi, with assistance from the CNT member, was able to talk her out of the residence. The woman specifically requested to go to detox as she had relapsed with drinking alcohol. Once she exited the residence, deputies placed her in protective custody under the Baker Act.
💚 During another incident, BRU Mental Health Specialist, Teresa Shaw, assisted deputies at a trespassing call involving a schizophrenic man that was having an episode. Shaw was able to de-escalate the individual, enroll him in mental health services, and has since been following up with him. During initial contact, it was discovered the subject did not have insurance and was not receiving any psychiatric services. Today, he is now stable, insured, on medication, and has housing.
